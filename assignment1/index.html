DAOST Chapter 2 Questions

Purpose of a jitter plot.

The purpose of a jitter plot is to separate data-sets that occupies the same space. By doing this we get a better view of how much of the data occupies the same space. There is no correlation between the distance of the points on the y-axis.

The purpose of figure 2-3

Figure 2-3 from DAOST - shown below - illustrates two different historams visualizing the same (relatively small) dataset.

The purpose of this figure is to show that no two histograms are created equal: When working with small data sets, not only do we need to carefully consider how many bins the histogram is constructed with, but also how the bins are alligned on the x-axis.
Small data sets are prone to being skewed by bin allignment especially with non-gaussian distribution. The figure caption might as well be how not to histogram.

When can KDEs be misleading?

Kernel Density Estimates (KDEs) are conceivably better than histograms at representing and retaining the integrity of certain data sets. Handling outliers, lack of bin size / allignement and unabridged representation gives them an advantage over histograms in many cases.   • What are residuals? Why is it a good idea to plot the residuals of your fit?
    • The residuals are difference between the true target values and the approximations. I.e. the error. It is a good idea to plot the residuals to see if:
      • the residuals are symmetrically distributed around zero.
      • the residuals are free of trend or structure. If there is a trend in the residuals, it suggests that the model fails to capture some information.
      • the residuals have constant variance and a mean around zero.
      • the residuals are normally distributed.
